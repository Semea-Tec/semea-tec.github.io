# SEMEA-tec: seeding tech, growing autonomy

## About the project

**SEMEA-TEC** is an outreach project at UNIFESP — São José dos Campos campus (ICT), approved by the Extension and Culture Chamber, operating at the intersection of computing, electronics, rural extension, and participatory methodologies. Our purpose is to **develop low-cost, low-energy social technologies** for family farming, tackling the digital divide that deepens historical inequalities in the countryside.

The project is organically linked to an ongoing doctoral research at PPGIT/UNIFESP, titled *"Sustainable Energy and Efficient Communication: Application of Harvest Energy Batteries and Low-Power Transmitters as Social Technology"*. While the outreach project operates as a living lab for listening and co-creation with communities, the doctoral research investigates the technical-scientific foundations for these systems to achieve maximum energy autonomy — using batteries that harvest energy from the environment based on non-toxic, low-impact materials — and economic viability at scale.

## How we work

We adopt **Action Design Research (ADR)** as our central methodology: before writing a single line of code or soldering anything, we go to the field with our partner organizations for **active listening and participatory diagnosis** of real needs. Only then do we move to co-creating prototypes, field-testing on farms, and iterative improvement cycles. Family farmers are at the center of the debate — not as passive beneficiaries, but as co-producers of technological innovation.

Our theoretical foundation is anchored in the **Epistemologies of the South** (Boaventura de Sousa Santos), **Social Technology** (Dagnino, Thomas), and a commitment to a just ecological transition, aligning our work with SDGs 1, 4, 7, 9, and 13. We also use the methodological framework of Pozzebon and Diniz (2012) for ICT in community contexts, which provides a multilevel, pluralistic, and remixable approach.

## Partnerships

The work is carried out with UNIFESP undergraduate students applying knowledge in computing, electronics, and sustainability in a real-world context, in coordination with organizations working daily in the field:

- **Associação Portal Sem Porteiras** (Monteiro Lobato - SP) — logistical support, mobilization of family farmers, and sharing of local knowledge.
- **Akarui** (São Luiz do Paraitinga - SP) — working on an ecological and economic restoration project integrating milk production with forest (Dec/2024 to Jun/2028).
- **Associação Apoena** — working in agroecology in the territory.

## Expected impact

Functional prototypes with open documentation, training workshops for farmers and students, didactic booklets in accessible language, production of scientific articles and experience reports. On the horizon, the articulation between outreach and doctoral research points toward an ecosystem of decentralized innovation: communication and sensing systems combining low-power radios (LoRa, Si44xx, EZRadioPro), lightweight protocols (IEEE 802.15.4), harvest-energy power supply, and participatory development methodologies — building not just technology, but technical autonomy and digital sovereignty for rural communities.

All outputs, including prototypes, manuals, and booklets, will be made available on open-access platforms and shared with communities at in-person meetings, extension fairs, and social technology forums.
