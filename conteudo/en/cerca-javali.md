# Wild Boar Fence — Voltage Monitoring via LoRa

## Context

Wild boar (*Sus scrofa*) invasions in family farming areas are a serious problem in the Paraíba Valley and across many regions of Brazil. These animals destroy entire crops in a single night, compromising food security and the income of farming families. Electric fences are the main defense, but their effectiveness depends on constant maintenance: a simple voltage failure opens the way for an attack.

It was from this concrete demand, brought by partner organizations during the participatory diagnosis, that we launched the first round of SEMEA-TEC.

## Solution architecture

We developed a remote monitoring system for electric fence voltage, with communication via LoRa radio. The prototype architecture combines:

### Sensing

The signal from an electric fence is complex: high-voltage pulses (in the kV range), of short duration and with an irregular waveform. In the first iteration, we attempted to capture the signal via coil induction, but the approach proved ineffective — the captured signal did not faithfully represent the fence pulses, showing only interference and grounding issues.

The solution came through two complementary approaches:

- **Hall effect sensor**: detects the magnetic field generated by the fence pulse, providing an indirect but robust reading of current flow.
- **Dedicated fence voltmeter**: a specific instrument for measuring the actual voltage at the terminals, providing a direct and reliable measurement.

This combination allowed us to correctly interpret fence operation, distinguishing normal operation from failure events.

### Processing and communication

- **Microcontroller**: responsible for reading the sensors and managing the radio.
- **LoRa transceiver**: operating on the ISM 915 MHz band (regulated in Brazil), with chirp spread spectrum (CSS) modulation, offering excellent range in rural areas and high noise immunity.
- **Custom acknowledgment protocol**: we implemented a simple handshake — the transmitter sends the voltage drop alert and waits for acknowledgment from the receiver. If the receiver is offline (momentarily out of range, powered off), the transmitter retransmits the alert until confirmation is received. This ensures that a fence failure never goes unnoticed.

### Communication link

The link is a **point-to-point simplex with acknowledgment**, prioritizing reliability over throughput — suitable for the sporadic traffic profile typical of failure events (order of magnitude: a few bytes per event, a few events per week).

## Current status and next steps

**Completed:**
- Correct reading and interpretation of the fence signal (Hall effect + voltmeter).
- Communication protocol with handshake and retransmission.
- Functional point-to-point LoRa communication.

**In progress / next cycle:**
- Integration of all modules into a single field device.
- Case design for weather protection (sun, rain, dust).
- Bootloader optimization to allow remote firmware updates.
